Fried Fish Tacos are a delicious combination of crispy, golden-brown fried fish topped with a creamy, spicy sauce. Each taco is garnished with tangy pickled red onions and fiery habanero peppers, offering a perfect balance of flavors and textures. Fresh and zesty, these tacos are a delightful treat for any meal.

Ingredients

For the Fish:
  • 1 lb white fish fillets (such as cod or tilapia)
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cornmeal
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Vegetable oil for frying
For the Creamy Spicy Sauce:
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1/4 cup sour cream
  • 2 tablespoons lime juice
  • 1 tablespoon hot sauce (such as Sriracha)
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt to taste
For the Pickled Red Onions and Habaneros:
  • 1 large red onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 habanero peppers, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
For Assembly:
  • 8 small corn tortillas
  • Shredded cabbage or lettuce
  • Fresh cilantro leaves
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  • Pickled Red Onions and Habaneros:

    1. Prepare the Pickling Liquid:

      In a small saucepan, combine vinegar, water, sugar, and salt. Bring to a boil, stirring to dissolve the sugar and salt.

    2. Pickle the Vegetables:

      Place the sliced red onions and habaneros in a heatproof jar or bowl. Pour the hot vinegar mixture over them. Let it sit at room temperature for at least 1 hour or refrigerate for longer to develop more flavor.

  • Creamy Spicy Sauce:

    1. Mix the Sauce Ingredients:

      In a small bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, sour cream, lime juice, hot sauce, and minced garlic. Season with salt to taste. Set aside.

  • Fish:

    1. Prepare the Fish:

      Pat the fish fillets dry with paper towels and cut them into taco-sized strips.

    2. Set Up Breading Station:

      In a shallow dish, combine flour, cornmeal, salt, pepper, and paprika. In another shallow dish, beat the eggs and mix in the milk.

    3. Bread the Fish:

      Dip each fish strip into the egg mixture, then dredge in the flour mixture, ensuring an even coating.

    4. Fry the Fish:

      Heat about 1/4 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Once the oil is hot, fry the fish strips until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side. Drain on paper towels.

  • Assembly:

    1. Warm the Tortillas:

      Warm the corn tortillas in a dry skillet or microwave.

    2. Assemble the Tacos:

      Place a few pieces of fried fish on each tortilla. Top with shredded cabbage or lettuce, a drizzle of the creamy spicy sauce, and some pickled red onions and habaneros.

    3. Garnish and Serve:

      Garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and serve with lime wedges on the side.
